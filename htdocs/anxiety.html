Anxiety is a normal struggle for all of us.

We feel anxious when we forget something, when we lose an item, or when we make important decisions. With anxiety being a common part of us, it is hard to break the stigma and just "brush it off" when we talk about different Anxiety Disorders. They always say that it hass no differemce to simple worries, but it's more than that. When they say normal anxiety, it goes away after a while, but for a person with an anxiety disorder, it usually doesn't go away that quick, and it can get drastically horrible as time passes by.

According to the Anxiety and Depression Association of America (ADAA), the term "anxiety disorder" refers to a specific type of psychologic disorder that centers in intense pain and worry. Things like panic disorders, phobias, social anxiety disorders, are just some disorders that fall under this category.

WebMD.com listed down the symptoms of anxiety disorders. While some aren't included, these are some that all anxiety disorders share.

  • Panic, fear, and uneasiness.
  • Sleep Problems
  • Fidgety Feelings (Not being able to sit still
  • Cold, sweaty, numb or tingling hands or feet
  • Shortness of Breath
  • Heart palpitations
  • Dry Mouth
  • Nausea
  • Tense muscles
  • Dizziness

19.1% of adults in the United States have an anxiety disorder. 7% of children aged 3-17 experience issues with anxiety annually. Most people develop symptoms before turning 21. In addition, according to the World Health Organization (WHO), 1 in 13 globally suffers from anxiety. The WHO reports that anxiety disorders are the most common mental disorders worldwide with specific phobia, major depressive disorder and social phobia being the most common anxiety disorders. (facts derived from ADAA.org & NAMI.org)

NAMI.org listed down the types of anxiety disorders. These are the most common.


Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

GAD is capable of producing constant worry in everyday life. This worrying can last for a full a day which makes the patient incapable of concentrating on normal tasks. They are commonly experiencing headaches, tension, or nausea.

Social Anxiety Disorder

This disorder induces fear of social interaction among its victims, often driven by worries of humiliation. People with Social Anxiety disorders tend to have panic attacks when forced to social interaction.

Panic Disorders

Panic disorders are often mistaken for heart attacks, as it causes physical symptoms like chest pain, heart palpitations, dizziness, shortness of breath, and stomach upset. These are characterized by panic attacks and sudden bouts of terror that strike repeatedly without warning

Phobias

People tend to be scared of things that make us scared, terrified. People with phobias, however, have much more different greivances. Phobias tend to give people powerful reactions of strong, irrational fear when confronted with certain places, events, or objects. Examples would be globophobia (fear of balloons), arichibutyrophobia(fear of peanut butter sticking to the roof of your tongue) and many bizzare ones.

Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

OCD is defined as a long-lastic, chronic, and common disorder wherein a person has reoccuring obsessions and compulsions in which they are urged to constantly do over and over. Some examples of compulsions and obsessions are symmetrical alignment of things, excessive hand washing, aggresive self-harming thoughts, constant check-up on random things, and more.

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

PTSD refers to a MH condition that is triggered by any event one has witnessed or experienced. When these occur, the patient tends to have symptoms of irrepressible memories, nightmares, and can interefere with day-to-day functioning. Most people have difficulty coping, but fortunately, this condition can be cured through long-term medication and healthcare.

Other Anxiety Disorders

Other disorders include:

  • Agoraphobia (fear of social interaction)
  • Selective mutism (people fail to speak specific social situations despite normal laguage skills)

  • SepAnx (Separation Anxiety)
  • Substance/medication-induced anxiety disorders (usually involving intoxication or withdrawal or medication treatment

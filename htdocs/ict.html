Solution 1:



Apps

Apps that range from Eating Disorders to Schizophrenia are mostly beneficial due to convenience, anonymity, and a low price. The problem emerges when user-patients use these apps for stand-in to actual medical treatment. Most applications lack peer-reviewed research, partly because tech companies improve quicker than traditional scientific testing. Although these apps lack scientific backing, another research shows an improvement, or a positive result from the user-patients. We also recommend visiting https://www.psycom.net/25-best-mental-health-apps to find certain apps to help you.





Solution 2:



Online Videos (for Therapy)

Online Therapy through Video seems to be a good solution for patients due to the shortage of mental health professionals. One of its upsides is the fact that this is efficient for people who lack the resources to have an actual appointment in-person, or are simply, quite afraid of the stigma.





Solution 3:



Behavior Tracking Software

Behavior Tracker Apps create digital phenotypes or detectors based on your interaction (with consent and permission) through cell phones, such as not responding to texts, and analyzes it. Not responding to texts of friends and family could be a sign of depression or an increase in shopping spree would be an indicator of mania.





Solution 4:



Medical Recording Software

Medical Recording Software are being used by insurance companies to identify patients at risk for mental illnesses. After this, they are treated through a network of mental health specialists.





Solution 5:



VR Treatment

VR Treatment has been used for exposure therapy to treat many who have PTSD. Exposure therapy is when patients are made to relive their trauma in a safe environment. VR is also used to understand and cope borderline personality disorder, wherein people have difficulty controlling their emotions.





Solution 6:



Depression Tests

Depression Tests have been made by Google in coordination with the National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) in order to determine depression. Other than that, there are also tests online such as this one from https://depression.org.nz/is-it-depression-anxiety/self-test/ that can help you detect signs of depression or anxiety.
